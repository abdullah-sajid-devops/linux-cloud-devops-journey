# Day 23 of Cisco Linux Essentials Course

Today I learned about private cloud servers — which projects power them, and honestly, the most interesting part was understanding why organizations actually need them.

## Private Cloud Servers
This is software that companies deploy on their own servers and manage themselves. The biggest advantage here is that your data never goes to a third-party company — it stays right within your own office or server room, basically creating a setup similar to Google Drive, but fully private.

## ownCloud Project
This project was launched in 2010 by Frank Karlitschek to provide software that lets you store, sync, and share data from private cloud servers. It's available under the standard open-source GNU AGPLv3 license, and there's also a commercial license that comes with more features.

## Nextcloud Project
Nextcloud was forked from ownCloud in 2016, also by Karlitschek, and has been growing rapidly ever since. It also falls under the GNU AGPLv3 license, and its goal is to maintain an open and transparent development process.

## The Purpose Behind Both Projects
Both of these projects are focused entirely on providing private cloud software that meets the needs of organizations — big or small — where security, privacy, and data control matter.
